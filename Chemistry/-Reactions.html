Physical & Chemical Reactions

There are several different types of reactions and they can be catagorized in many ways. There are mainly two types of reactions, physical reactions and chemical reactions.

Physical reactions are those reactions in which no new substance is formed. For example, when water is converted into ice, no new substance is formed. The water is still water, just in a different state. Moreover, physical reactions can be reversed.

Chemical reactions are those reactions in which new substances are formed. For example, when hydrogen and oxygen are combined, they form water.

This is a chemical equation:

A + B ‚Üí C + D

Chemical equations are used to represent chemical reactions. In this reaction, A and B are the reactants and C and D are the products.The arrow indicates the direction of the reaction. The reactants are usually placed on the left and the products are placed on the right. The properties of A & B differ from those of C & D.

Chemical reactions can be classified into different types based on the type of reaction that occurs. We will be discussing furthur about the different types of chemical reactions in the next chapter.

üóí Note:
There is a third type of reaction called nuclear reactions. These are reactions that involve changes in the nucleus of an atom. They are different from chemical reactions because they involve changes in the nucleus of an atom, while chemical reactions involve changes in the electron cloud. Nuclear reactions are responsible for the energy production in the sun and nuclear power plants.
